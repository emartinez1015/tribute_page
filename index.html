Linus Torvalds

A Linux man

Here's a time line of Linus Torvalds life:

  • 1969 - Torvalds was born in Helsinki, Finland
  • 1981 - at the age of 11, initially programming in BASIC, but later by directly accessing the 6502 CPU in machine code.
  • 1988 - 1996 - Torvalds attended the University of Helsinki, graduating with a master's degree in computer science
  • 1991 - Torvalds first encountered the GNU Project, after another Swedish-speaking computer science student, Lars Wirzenius, took him to the University of Technology to listen to free software guru Richard Stallman's speech.
  • 1996 - Torvalds accepted a position at the company in California, where he would work from February 1997 until June 2003
  • 1997 - he was involved in 86open helping to choose the standard binary format for Linux and Unix.
  • 1999 - Red Hat and VA Linux, both leading developers of Linux-based software, presented Torvalds with stock options in gratitude for his creation
  • 2008 - Torvalds stated that he used the Fedora distribution of Linux because it had fairly good support for the PowerPC processor architecture, which he had favored at the time
  • 2018 - the Linux Kernel Code of Conflict was suddenly replaced by a new Code of Conduct based on the Contributor Covenant
